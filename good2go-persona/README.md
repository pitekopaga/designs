### [Alex Johnson](./alex.jpg) - The Non-Resident Business Traveler
*"I just want to pay my toll and forget about it. Why does it have to be so complicated?"*

**Role**: Business Consultant from Portland traveling to Seattle 4-6 times/year
**Key Needs**:
- One-time toll payments without residency complications
- Instant digital receipts for expense reimbursement
- Cross-state compatibility (Oregon plates in Washington)

**Design Implications**:
- Mobile-first payment interface
- Receipt generation with proper accounting data
- Clear out-of-state user onboarding

### [Maya Chen](./maya.jpg) - The Daily Commuter
*"I just need it to work. I don't have time at 7 AM to wonder if my toll was paid or if my Flex Pass is set right."*

**Role**: Marketing Manager commuting I-405, switching between solo and family HOV travel
**Key Needs**:
- Automated HOV mode switching
- Predictable monthly costs
- Proactive alerts for mode changes

**Design Implications**:
- Schedule-based HOV automation
- Predictive billing notifications
- Simple family account management

## ðŸŽ¯ Persona Development Methodology

These personas were created following industry best practices:

1. **Segment identification** - Distinct user groups based on usage patterns
2. **Research synthesis** - Behavioral specificity over demographics
3. **Actionable insights** - Each persona generates concrete design questions
4. **Cross-functional utility** - Useful for product, engineering, marketing, support
